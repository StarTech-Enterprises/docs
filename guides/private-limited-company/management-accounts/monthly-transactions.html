    Monthly Transactions

    Guides • Private Limited Company • 15/11/2019

    Overview

    The monthly transaction tabs are where (almost) all daily and weekly transactions should be recorded. There are 12 of them, one for each month.

    Tax Point Date

    Important:

    Rules: Tax Point Date: There are special rules from HMRC about what this date should be set as, depending on whether a service or good was provided, and depending on whether the invoice was raised in advance or arrears.

    In general, the Tax Point Date should be set as the earlier of: the Date of Cash payment, or Date of Invoice (whichever is earlier). If an Invoice is issued in arrears, and is 15 days after the date of supply, the Tax Point Date should be the Date of supply.

    Tax Point Date Rules

    The rules (as of the time of writing this article!) are summarised here:

    In any supply, there are four relevant stages to bear in mind: 1) making goods available, 2) completion of a service, 3) invoicing, 4) payment:

    Situation Tax Point
    No invoice needed Date of supply
    VAT invoice issued Date of invoice
    VAT invoice issued 15 days or more after the date of supply Date supply took place
    Payment in advance of supply and no VAT invoice yet issued Date payment received
    Payment or invoice issued in advance of supply Date of payment or invoice (whichever is earlier)

    The Date of Supply:

    • For goods – the date they are sent, collected, or made available (e.g. installed in the customer’s house)
    • For services – the date the work is finished

    Additional Points:

    • As a buyer, you cannot reclaim input VAT, without a valid VAT invoice
    • If you pay a supplier invoice in advance, you cannot reclaim the VAT element of the payment without a valid VAT invoice
    • If you reclaim input VAT on a supplier’s invoice, but fail to pay the supplier within 6 months, you usually have to repay the VAT.

    HMRC:
    But check the latest HMRC guidance here: VAT Record Keeping

    Purchasing Agent

    Note:
    If you’re buying services and goods yourself for the company (e.g. as a Director), we’ve considered these transactions to have been made in the capacity of a purchasing agent.

    Buying Agent

    Buying as an agent simply means you have your own account with the company, so that settlements can be properly recorded and tracked.

    Any cash payments made by the company to settle such agent purchases, would be recorded as ‘Co. Bank Payment’, with the ‘Expense Reimbursement’ option chosen under the Analysis field.

    More information

    More information about buying agents can be found on the Government’s own website here: Agents and VAT - HMRC
